About Me

I am a product developer and researcher at a tech education company based in London. In my day-to-day work, I write course materials for our BCS level 4 data analysis apprenticeship, and perform internal research on our curriculum content. In addition to my course writing and research, I create data visualisations and dashboards for internal and external use, and write for our company newsletter on topics relating to data ethics and data communication.

Originally from the suburbs of Chicago, USA, I moved to the London to pursue a doctorate in psychology, with a focus on developmental psychopathology. During my time, I was able to hone my statistical and analytical skills, using structural equation modelling to study possible links between early-life nutrition and behavioural development from epidemiological cohort data. When I completed my degree, I was interested in pursuing a career outside of academia, and instead using my research, writing, and analytical skills in a more practical setting. 

When I have spare time, I love to indulge my creative side, be it through painting, any sort of art project, or simply just reading a good novel.
